november // 2015

Five minutes into the album I was shaking my head and wondering, 'how did it take me this long to hear this!?' After that moment I had Camp on repeat for a solid two weeks.

I tend to gravitate towards smart/indie crossover rap—if that description even makes any sense—and this album hit that vein perfectly. I found really refreshing to hear Donald Glover (aka Childish Gambino) cater to the surging african american middle class, basically saying, 'who cares if you're not a gangster or grew up in Compton. And does that even matter?'

I also found that I could relate to a lot of Glover's material, growing up in the same era and seemingly were drawn to many of the same influences. For instance in Firefly when he says he was 'the only black kid at a Sufjan Concert' and in L.E.S. admitting he loves the Nikelodeon cartoon Rugrats. These are just a couple of the many golden nuggets in this album that left me smiling.

Please don't make the same mistake that I did and overlook this album and Childish Gambino's other material. Seriously, don't wait!

october // 2015

Tame Impala's new album Currents is exactly the kind of genius album that few bands create now-a-days. It fights directly against modern-day streaming's lack of incentive for artists to create a comprehensive album. It's an experience meant to be experienced in its fullness—not on a one-off basis. Every song in Currents is thematically important and is built and molded to be consumed together. If you're skimming this album for a killer intro song on your new playlist, look elsewhere.

Returning Tame Impala fans will find that Currents is much different from Lonerism and InnerSpeaker. As cliche as it is to say that a band's third album is 'more mature' and 'grown up'—it is. And it's amazing. It's lyrically compelling and personal and the instrumentals are more controlled, yet more psychedelic than ever before. You find yourself sinking deeper and deeper into its complexities after each listen. Seriously though, this album would fit right in with the late-night laser shows playing Dark Side of the Moon or Led Zeppelin.

Aside from the sounds and feelings of this album, I became even more enthralled with Tame Impala's mastermind Kevin Parker. After my first listen I almost obsessively sought out additional content and 'behind the scenes' information. I learned that Parker's writing and creating style is much the same as my own. The mindset is obsessive—and has to be to get it right. When asked about what music influenced and inspired his writing in Currents he said:

"It sounds like a cop-out, but I honestly couldn't name anything that directly influenced me, because I don't really listen to a lot of music these days, intentionally. I'm so consumed by making music myself that I just prefer to be thinking about new music that I make all the time. Especially when I'm in the writing and recording process, the idea of music in the outside world is a distraction almost."

in Midway, Utah.
