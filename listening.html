How I never discovered this album until now is a mystery. I had Camp on repeat for a solid two weeks.

I tend to gravitate towards smart/indie crossover rap--if that description even makes any sense--and this album hit that vein perfectly for me. I found it incredibly refreshing to hear Donald Glover (aka Childish Gambino) cater to the surging african american middle class, basically saying, 'who cares if you're not a gangster or grew up in Compton. Does that even matter?'

I also found that I could relate to a lot of Glover's material, since we grew up in the same era and seemingly were drawn to many of the same influences. For instance in Firefly when he says he was 'the only black kid at a Sufjan Concert' and in L.E.S. admitting he loves the Nikelodeon cartoon Rugrats. These are just a couple of the many golden nuggets in this album that left me smiling.
