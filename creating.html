DIY cable holder
October // 2015

I finally got frusterated enough from destroying several cables by rolling my chair over them that I decided to figure something out. I had an old iphone 5 case lying around and figured it'd be the perfect solution. It literally took 5 minutes to plan and 5 minutes to build. Here's what you gotta do:

items

1 - Old phone case: if it isn't a two-piece like I've shown you can cut your's into two pieces really easy.

1 - Velcro strip

tools

1 - Pair of scissors

step one

Cut the velcro strip in half and attach one piece to the back of the case (see above for location). Then attach the other velcro piece to the bottom of whatever you'd like to attach it to. I attached mine to the underside of my desk just above where my cords plug into my surge protector.

step two

Thread the cables through the opening in the phone case (usually easier before you velcro it to the other piece). Note that the opening in the phone case only allows cables with small heads through. For me that meant my iPhone charger, Beats/Android/Kindle charger, and then the audio cable for my monitor. After you've threaded the cables though simply push both velcro pieces together and 'voila' your cable destroying days are over!

MBR logo redesign
July // 2015

In the picture above, you will see the Missão Brasil Recife logo from my 2008 to 2010 generation. To my knowledge it remains the same. I decided to try my graphic design skills out and attempt a logo redesign.

Those who have served an LDS mission will likely be familiar with the picture above and to the left. This is the universal logo for 'baptism'. As missionaries we baptize a lot and talk about it even more and this logo makes it much easier to write down instead of writing the whole word out — at least that's how I used it.

During my mission I developed a slightly new style for it that helps represent what baptism is—seen above and to the right. First, you can see the waves representing 'baptism by water' - or full immersion. Second, is the sun which represents 'baptism by fire' or receiving the Holy Ghost. Lastly, the arrow represents eternal life or 'enduring to the end'. Put them all together, preceded by Faith and Repentance, and you have the Gospel of Jesus Christ.

For more information about the Gospel and Baptism according to Mormons click here.

After countless iterations and some serendipitous mistakes I created an updated logo that I feel mixes the Missão Brasil Recife and baptism logos well. The design is also very friendly to color and variation and also makes a great circular icon. I decided to leave it in its more simple form without text. I figure if it ever actually gets used, I can easily add it.
